## 1. Stakeholder Identification

### How to Identify Stakeholders

Ask these questions to ensure completeness:
- Who will be directly affected by the project outcome?
- Who controls budget, resources, or access needed for this project?
- Who has the authority to stop, change, or approve this project?
- Who has relevant expertise that this project depends on?
- Who represents customers, users, or end-beneficiaries?
- Who will need to change their behaviour, processes, or tools as a result?
- Who are the vocal critics or potential resistors?
- Who is politically important even if not directly involved?

## 3. Influence / Interest Matrix

> Place each stakeholder (using their ID) in the quadrant that best describes their position. This helps prioritise your engagement approach.

```
HIGH INTEREST
      |
      |   KEEP SATISFIED        |    MANAGE CLOSELY
      |   (High Influence,      |    (High Influence,
      |    Low Interest)        |     High Interest)
      |   S-01, S-04            |    S-02, S-03, S-07
      |                         |
------+-------------------------+------------------------
LOW   |   MONITOR               |    KEEP INFORMED
INFLU-|   (Low Influence,       |    (Low Influence,
ENCE  |    Low Interest)        |     High Interest)
      |   S-05, S-06            |    S-08
      |                         |
      +-------------------------+------------------------
                                         HIGH INTEREST
```

> **Note:** Update this diagram by replacing the example IDs (S-01, S-02...) with your actual stakeholder IDs based on your assessment.

**Engagement Strategy by Quadrant:**
- **Manage Closely (High Influence, High Interest):** Deepest engagement. Involve in decisions, seek input regularly, address concerns proactively.
- **Keep Satisfied (High Influence, Low Interest):** Periodic updates. Engage when decisions affect them. Do not overwhelm with detail.
- **Keep Informed (Low Influence, High Interest):** Regular communication. Provide transparency. Listen for signals of changing influence.
- **Monitor (Low Influence, Low Interest):** Minimal but courteous engagement. Watch for changes in their position.
