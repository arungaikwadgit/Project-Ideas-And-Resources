# Decision Log: [Project / Initiative Name]

> **Purpose:** The decision log is the authoritative record of all significant decisions made on this project or initiative. It captures what was decided, why, by whom, and what alternatives were considered. A well-maintained decision log prevents revisiting settled debates, supports onboarding, and provides audit trail for governance purposes.

## How to Use This Log

1. **Add decisions promptly** — record decisions as they are made, not days later when context is lost.
2. **Capture the rationale** — the "why" is more important than the "what." Future readers need to understand the reasoning, not just the outcome.
3. **Record alternatives** — document the options that were NOT chosen and why. This prevents re-opening settled debates.
4. **Identify the decision-maker** — distinguish between who was consulted and who made the final call.
5. **Link to supporting artefacts** — connect decisions to the PRDs, risk registers, or meeting notes that provide deeper context.
6. **Mark status** — update decisions when they are revisited, reversed, or superseded.
